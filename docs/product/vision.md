# Product Vision

## What Mobicare Is

Mobicare is a mobile app for Android and iOS that helps everyday drivers in Brazil take better care of their cars.

The product acts as an intelligent automotive maintenance assistant, helping users remember preventive maintenance, track vehicle costs, store maintenance history, and understand what needs to be done without requiring mechanical expertise.

## Current Hypotheses

- The first audience is the common driver, not fleet managers or professional drivers.
- The app should use simple Portuguese and avoid technical automotive jargon when possible.
- The first version should create value through reminders, maintenance history, and cost tracking before introducing advanced AI features.
- AI should initially operate only in the backend to enrich maintenance plans and automotive data.
- Vehicle plate, location-like usage patterns, receipts, and maintenance history may create privacy and compliance obligations.

## Target Users

- Brazilian drivers who own or regularly use one or more cars.
- Drivers who are not experts in automotive maintenance.
- Drivers who want to avoid forgetting preventive maintenance.
- Drivers who want to understand the real monthly and per-kilometer cost of owning a car.

## Core Problem

For everyday drivers in Brazil,
who need to maintain their cars but often do not understand what must be done or when,
Mobicare provides maintenance reminders, vehicle history, and cost tracking,
so they can avoid neglected maintenance, reduce surprises, and understand the cost of ownership.

## Success Criteria

- Users can register a vehicle and receive an initial maintenance plan.
- Users can record fuel, maintenance, mileage, and general costs with low friction.
- Users receive useful maintenance alerts based on mileage and time.
- Users can understand monthly cost, cost per kilometer, average consumption, and spend by category.
- The app avoids giving unsafe or unsupported mechanical recommendations.
- Sponsored recommendations are clearly identified.

## Product Principles

- Clarity over automotive depth.
- Trust before monetization.
- Reminders before diagnosis.
- Structured maintenance rules before AI-generated advice.
- Evidence and source tracking for AI-enriched automotive data.

## Open Questions

- Should the MVP support only cars or include motorcycles later?
- Should the MVP require plate number, or can plate be optional to reduce privacy friction?
- What vehicle data source will be used for make, model, year, version, and engine normalization?
- Will the app start with manual maintenance rules before using AI enrichment?
- What is the first monetization test: subscription, sponsorship, affiliate links, or free beta?
